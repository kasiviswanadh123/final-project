Cardiology

Cardiology is a specialized branch of medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases related to the heart and blood vessels.

It is a crucial field in healthcare, as cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) remain one of the leading causes of morbidity and mortality worldwide.

Cardio-Oncologist

Focuses on the diagnosis, prevention, and medical management of heart diseases. Uses non-invasive tests such as ECG, echocardiograms, and stress tests. Treats conditions like high blood pressure, heart failure, and arrhythmias with medications and lifestyle changes.

Electrophysiologist

Focuses on heart rhythm disorders (arrhythmias) like atrial fibrillation. Performs procedures like pacemaker implantation, defibrillator placement, and catheter ablation. Specializes in managing heart failure and advanced cardiac conditions. Provides medication therapy, implantable devices, and heart transplant referrals. Treats heart diseases and congenital heart defects in infants, children, and teenagers. Performs fetal echocardiograms to detect heart problems before birth. Frequently Asked Questions

How do you evaluate the performance of an ML model?

Performance is measured using metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and AUC-ROC curve.

Can AI predict heart disease?

Yes, machine learning models can analyze health data to predict the likelihood of heart disease with high accuracy.

How reliable is machine learning in detecting heart disease?

ML models can be quite reliable (80â€“90% accuracy), but they should be used as an aid to doctors, not as a replacement.

Can I use an app or website to check my heart disease risk?

Some apps and online tools use ML to assess heart disease risk, but results should always be confirmed with a doctor.

What information do I need to provide for an ML model to predict my heart disease risk?

Common inputs include age, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, heart rate, smoking history, diabetes status, and ECG results.

Does machine learning predict heart attacks?

ML can estimate the risk of heart disease and conditions that lead to heart attacks, but it cannot predict the exact time of an attack.
